# Hush v0.1: Product Foundation Document — Part 17: UX/UI Specification

---

## 344. UX Philosophy
The user experience must shift the mental model from "Messaging = Storage" to "Messaging = Experience." The goal is to make users feel safe, calm, in control, and intentional.

## 345. Design Principles
* **Calm Over Addictive:** Minimize noise, eliminate engagement loops, and avoid unnecessary notifications.
* **Trust Through Clarity:** Security must be understandable, not mysterious.
* **Meaningful Actions:** Key moments (Starting, Verifying, Ending) must be intentional.
* **Minimalist Interface:** The conversation is the focus; the interface should be nearly invisible.

## 346. Brand Identity
* **Personality:** Quiet, elegant, trustworthy, and human.
* **Voice:** Simple, clear, and respectful. No technical jargon.
* **Visuals:** Avoid aggressive security or hacker aesthetics. Prioritize calm, sophisticated neutrals with deep contrast.

## 347. App Architecture
* **Navigation:** Flat structure consisting of: Conversations, Contacts, Identity, and Settings.
* **Screens:** Focused on 8 core screens (Welcome, Identity, Home, Contacts, Conversation, Settings, Completion Confirmation, Destroyed Screen).

## 348. The Signature UX (The Lifecycle)
* **Creation:** Seamless, low-friction onboarding.
* **Security Indicator:** Visible, accessible "Private" badge that explains the model on tap.
* **Completion UX:** An intentional "End Hush" flow that requires confirmation, followed by a peaceful, meaningful dissolution animation.
* **Empty States:** Designed to teach, not to trigger anxiety. "Nothing here because nothing needs to remain."

## 349. Accessibility & Motion
* **Accessibility:** Full support for screen readers, large text, high contrast, and reduced motion.
* **Motion Design:** Animations exist only to communicate the beginning, transition, or natural ending of a moment. Avoid unnecessary flair.

## 350. Success Metrics (UX Focus)
* **Understanding:** Can the user explain the "why" of Hush after onboarding?
* **Confidence:** Does the user trust the destruction model?
* **Completion Rate:** Are users successfully reaching the "end" of their conversations?
