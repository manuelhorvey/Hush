# Hush v0.1: Product Foundation Document — Part 12: Operating Manual

---

## 228. Founder Mindset
The core responsibility is not just building features, but protecting the original philosophy—ensuring the product remains a tool for intentional communication rather than an engine for accumulation and surveillance.

## 229. The Hush Decision Filter
Every major decision must pass these criteria:
1. Does it strengthen privacy? (If no, reject).
2. Does it make communication more human?
3. Would we be comfortable explaining this publicly?
4. Does it create unnecessary data? (If yes, redesign).
5. Does it help Hush become the category leader?

## 230. Product Constitution
* **Article 1:** Privacy is a requirement, not a feature.
* **Article 2:** Collect only the minimum necessary data.
* **Article 3:** Security decisions require evidence, not assumptions.
* **Article 4:** Simplicity beats complexity.
* **Article 5:** Trust is more valuable than short-term revenue.

## 231. Engineering & Culture
* **Removal Culture:** Always ask, "What can we remove?" to reduce clutter and data footprints.
* **Deep Thinking:** Prioritize written documentation and long-form thought over endless meetings.
* **Collective Responsibility:** Everyone (designers, engineers, managers) is a guardian of user privacy.

## 232. Handling Mistakes
A privacy company cannot hide errors. Acknowledge quickly, explain clearly, fix transparently, and improve systems. Trust is solidified by how failures are managed.

## 233. Metrics Philosophy
Optimize for **Trust, Quality, and Privacy**, not engagement metrics like "time spent in app." A 10-minute conversation that leaves no trace is a success, not a failure.

## 234. The Founder’s Hardest Challenge
Maintaining simplicity in the face of constant requests for feature bloat (AI, social feeds, stories). The founder must remain the guardian of the product's "ephemeral" soul.

## 235. The Hush Legacy
To change how the world thinks about digital communication: moving from a mindset where "everything online is permanent" to one where "digital moments can have a natural ending."
